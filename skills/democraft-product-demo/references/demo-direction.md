# Demo direction

Use this after Discovery and before authoring or repairing. The goal is a clear
product walkthrough, not a transcript of mouse movement.

## Narrative rules

- One demo has one primary objective.
- Every scene answers one viewer question.
- Start by establishing context or showing the outcome.
- Show the result of an important action before moving to the next action.
- End in a stable, understandable final state.
- Captions explain product value, not obvious cursor motion.
- Remove actions that do not serve the objective.

## Visual rules

- Do not focus every click.
- Establish the page before the first close focus when context matters.
- Avoid moving camera while the viewer needs to read.
- Avoid leaving the camera zoomed while jumping between distant regions.
- Do not place callouts or captions over the target being explained.
- Prefer one highlight/callout/caption idea at a time.

## Attention budget

Avoid changing all attention channels at once. In one short interval, try to use
only one or two of:

- camera movement;
- cursor movement;
- caption change;
- callout introduction;
- highlight/focus change.

If a scene feels busy, remove a focus step first, then shorten or split the
caption.
